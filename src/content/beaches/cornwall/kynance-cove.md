description: Serpentine stacks, white sand and turquoise water — the most photographed cove in Cornwall, on the National Trust's Lizard. Dogs are welcome most of the year on a lead, with only the short summer ban to plan around. The one non-negotiable is the tide, which fills the coves fast and strands people every single year, so this is a check-the-tide-table beach before anything else.
