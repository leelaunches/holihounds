name: Porthtowan
region: cornwall
location: Near St Agnes
description: A dramatic surf beach on the St Agnes heritage coast, hemmed by dark cliffs and old mine workings, with the well-loved dog-friendly Blue Bar looking straight out over the sand. It carries the longer summer ban as a Seaside Award beach, so July and August are early-and-late affairs — but out of season it's one of the moodiest, best dog walks on the north coast, and the surf is serious.
dog_access: seasonal
dog_restriction: Summer daytime ban (Seaside Award beach)
restriction_detail: Dogs banned 15 May–30 September, 10am–6pm under Cornwall Council's beach order (the longer Seaside Award band). Allowed all other dates, and before 10am / after 6pm even in summer. Dates are reviewed yearly — check the beach signage.
safety_note: A powerful surf beach with rip currents and a fast tide that cuts off the base of the cliffs — don't linger below them on a making tide. Lifeguarded in season; swim between the flags.
parking: Beachfront car park (pay-and-display; fills early in summer)
