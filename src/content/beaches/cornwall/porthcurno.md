description: A wedge of near-white shell sand and jade water below the open-air Minack Theatre, at the far western tip of Cornwall — a jaw-dropping spot, and dog-friendly outside the short summer daytime ban. It shelves steeply into strong water, so it's a paddle-and-explore beach rather than a swim with a dog. Pair it with the coast path to Logan Rock and the dog-friendly Logan Rock Inn at Treen.
dog_access: seasonal

safety_note: Steep-shelving white sand with strong currents and a fast tide — not a safe swim in any surf, and easy to be caught out by the depth close in. Lifeguarded in peak season only. Steep steps down and up.
