name: Perranporth
region: cornwall
location: Near Newquay
description: Three miles of surf, sand and dunes on the mid-north coast, and one of Cornwall's genuine year-round dog beaches — because even when the central lifeguarded stretch is restricted in summer, the vast dunes and the quieter northern end past the rocks stay open. It's also home to The Watering Hole, the beach bar out on the sand. A brilliant dog walk out of season, and workable even in August if you pick your spot and your hour.
dog_access: partial
dog_restriction: Dog-friendly year-round, with the central beach restricted in summer
restriction_detail: Dogs are welcome here all year on the dunes and the quieter northern end past the rocks. The central lifeguarded beach carries a summer restriction — reported both as an on-lead rule and as a 15 May–30 September daytime ban, and sources differ, so check the beach signage or Cornwall Council before you travel. Before 10am and after 6pm the whole beach is dog-friendly.
safety_note: Big Atlantic surf beach with rip currents — swim between the RNLI flags in season. A fast incoming tide covers a lot of sand, so don't get cut off below the cliffs at the northern end.
parking: Large beachfront and cliff-top car parks (pay-and-display)
