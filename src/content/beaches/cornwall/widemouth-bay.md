description: A mile of open sand just south of Bude on the far north coast — one of the region's big, easygoing surf-and-family beaches, with rock pools at low tide for a nosy dog. It takes the longer summer band, so peak-season days mean early or late walks, but the shoulder seasons are quiet and wide open. The Bay View Inn looks straight over the beach and welcomes dogs.
dog_access: seasonal
dog_restriction: Summer daytime ban (extended season)
restriction_detail: Dogs banned 15 May–30 September, 10am–6pm under Cornwall Council's beach order (the longer band). Allowed all other dates, and before 10am / after 6pm even in summer. Dates are reviewed yearly — check the beach signage.
safety_note: A mile of exposed Atlantic surf with strong rip currents — keep to the RNLI flags in season. Rocks and pools at low tide give way to a fast cover at high; watch the tide.
