name: St Mawes (Summers Beach)
region: cornwall
location: Roseland Peninsula
description: A small, sheltered village beach on the Roseland, tucked below St Mawes with calm water and a view across to the castle. Summers Beach takes the standard summer ban, but the Roseland out of season is one of Cornwall's gentlest dog-walking corners — sheltered coves, subtropical gardens and the King Harry Ferry for exploring. A pretty, low-key base rather than a big surf day, and all the better for a quiet dog.
dog_access: seasonal
dog_restriction: Summer daytime ban
restriction_detail: Dogs banned on Summers Beach 1 July–31 August, 10am–6pm under Cornwall Council's beach order. Allowed all other dates, and before 10am / after 6pm even in summer. Dates are reviewed yearly — check the beach signage.
safety_note: Sheltered estuary-side water with boat traffic and moorings — calmer than the open coast, but mind the channel and the tide.
