description: A showpiece triple flight of falls on the River Ure over about a mile of wooded Wensleydale, run by the national park — scenic, family-friendly and a fine dogs-on-leads walk. It's a paddling spot at the calmer stretches rather than a swim — the falls themselves are not safe, and after rain the Ure turns powerful with hidden undercurrents. Worth knowing too — campaigners have warned of high bacteria levels here, so it's not the spot to let a dog drink deeply.
