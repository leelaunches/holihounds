# Verified 18 Jun 2026. River Ribble near Settle. Deep pool, dangerous in high water + salmon run Oct-Nov.
name: Stainforth Force
region: yorkshire-dales
location: Stainforth, near Settle
description: A wide cascade on the River Ribble beside a 17th-century National Trust packhorse bridge, with a deep pool below it — dramatic, and a confident-swimmer's spot rather than a casual paddle. For dogs, the shallow margins are fine; keep them out of the main pool in high water. Two timing notes — avoid it in or after heavy rain when the flow churns, and in the autumn salmon run (October–November) it becomes a watch-don't-swim spectacle as fish leap the falls.
dog_access: lake-shore
dog_restriction: No seasonal ban — lead near farmland
restriction_detail: No seasonal closure; lead on the farmland approach. Beside an NT packhorse bridge.
safety_note: Deep pool, slippery limestone ledges — shallow margins only for dogs, and keep them out of the pool in high water. Avoid after heavy rain and during the autumn salmon run (Oct–Nov).
parking: National Park car park in Stainforth (with toilets), then a ~10 min downhill walk; parking at the force itself is very limited
