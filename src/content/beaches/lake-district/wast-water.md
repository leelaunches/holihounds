name: Wast Water

description: The wild one — a shingle-and-scree shore beneath the Scafell range at the dramatic, far-western end of the Lakes, with the lowest algae risk of any lake here thanks to its remote, cold, low-nutrient water. The trade-off is the depth, and it's a serious one — this is England's deepest lake, and the water drops away steeply right from the shore. Wonderful for a confident swimmer, a real hazard for a dog that over-commits.
