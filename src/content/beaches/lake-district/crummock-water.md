description: One of the calmest, least-visited shores on this list — a quiet shingle beach reached on a half-mile woodland walk from the Lanthwaite Wood car park, opening onto a dramatic view straight down the lake to a little old boathouse. National Trust land, no dog ban, and genuinely peaceful. The path is rocky and slippery in places, so boots over flip-flops, but the reward is solitude a dog can run off.
dog_access: lake-shore

safety_note: Crummock is a reservoir — keep away from the dams and equipment at the north end. It has appeared on algae bloom lists in past years, so check the water. Shelves deep fairly quickly; cold.
