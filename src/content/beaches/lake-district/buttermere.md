name: Buttermere
region: lake-district

description: A small beach and shallow shelf at the head of one of the most beautiful lakes in the country, with the classic four-and-a-half-mile shoreline circuit that's among the best round-the-lake dog walks anywhere. Quieter than the honeypot lakes, and a cleaner, higher, deeper lake that carries a far lower algae risk than Windermere or Coniston. A proper Lakeland choice rather than a facilitied one.
dog_access: lake-shore

restriction_detail: National Trust valley, open access. Lead around livestock and in ground-nesting-bird season (roughly March–July). No seasonal closure.
safety_note: Lower algae risk than the low-lying lakes, but still glance at the water — neighbouring Crummock has logged blooms in past years. Cold, and deepens away from the head.
