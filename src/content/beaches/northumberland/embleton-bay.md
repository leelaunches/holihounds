# Dunstanburgh Castle at the south end. Near Embleton/Craster.
name: Embleton Bay
region: northumberland
location: Near Craster
description: A curving dune-backed bay with the ruin of Dunstanburgh Castle standing at its southern end — one of the great coast walks in England, and dog-friendly all year. It's quieter than Bamburgh, National Trust-cared-for, and the walk from Craster past the castle and onto the sand is the classic way in. Big skies, few people, and a dog off the lead on empty sand for as far as you can see.
dog_access: year-round
dog_restriction: Year-round — no seasonal ban
restriction_detail: No summer dog ban — dogs are welcome all year on this stretch. Keep them under control near the dunes and the golf course behind, and watch for any roped-off areas protecting nesting birds. Check the signage on arrival.
safety_note: An open North Sea beach with rip currents and a fast tide; cold water and no year-round lifeguard, so take care with a swimming dog. The rocks towards Dunstanburgh cut off on a making tide.
parking: Car park at Embleton links / golf club, plus parking in Craster (pay-and-display)
