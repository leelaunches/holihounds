name: Beadnell Bay
region: northumberland
location: Near Seahouses
description: A wide, gently curving bay of pale sand — a watersports favourite in summer and a glorious dog walk the rest of the time. Dogs are welcome year-round, with one important caveat that does the coast credit — a stretch of the bay is a breeding ground for little terns, and dogs must be on a lead through the nesting season to protect them. Handle that and it's a big, easy, beautiful beach with our lodges a short drive inland.
dog_access: partial
dog_restriction: Year-round, but on-lead in the nesting area over summer
restriction_detail: Dogs are welcome all year — not banned — but a marked section of the bay is a protected little-tern breeding site, and dogs must be kept on a lead there through the nesting season (roughly May–August). Respect the roped-off areas and the signage; the rest of the bay is open as usual.
safety_note: Open North Sea bay with rip currents, a fast tide and cold water; no year-round lifeguard. Watersports craft launch here in summer, so keep a swimming dog clear of the launch area.
