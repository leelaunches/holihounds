# Beach guides confirm off-lead all year. Near Amble/Cresswell.
name: Druridge Bay
region: northumberland
location: Near Amble
description: Seven miles of unbroken sand — one of the longest beaches in England, dog-friendly and off-lead all year. Backed by a country park and Ladyburn Lake, it's so vast it never feels busy even in August, which makes it about the most reliable off-lead dog beach anywhere on the English coast. If you want space above all else, this is the one, and it's the closest of the great beaches to our Felmoor lodge.
dog_access: year-round
dog_restriction: Year-round, off-lead — no seasonal ban
restriction_detail: No summer dog ban, and dogs are welcome off the lead year-round where it's safe and they're under control. The scale of the place means there's always empty sand. Keep clear of the dunes and any fenced conservation areas behind the beach, and check the signage.
safety_note: Open North Sea surf with rip currents and a fast tide over flat sand; cold water and no year-round lifeguard. The sheer size means help can be far off, so keep a swimming dog close.
