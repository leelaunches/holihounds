location: Near Embleton
description: A tiny, National Trust-owned square of whitewashed cottages opening straight onto a quiet curve of sand — one of the most charming spots on the coast, and dog-friendly all year. The Ship Inn on the green brews its own beer and welcomes dogs, and Embleton Bay and Dunstanburgh are a walk along the shore. One thing to respect — the tern colony towards Long Nanny to the north is fenced and off-limits in the breeding season.
dog_access: year-round

restriction_detail: No summer dog ban on the main beach — dogs welcome all year. But north towards Long Nanny there's an important little-tern colony that's fenced off and must be avoided in the breeding season (roughly May–August); keep dogs out of the roped areas and on a lead near them. Check the signage.
